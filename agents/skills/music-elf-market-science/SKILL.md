# Music Elf Market Science

## Overview

Use this skill to turn market questions into current, sourced, testable product
decisions without hard-coding market assumptions into the core software.

## Research Rules

- Browse current sources for present-day competitor, pricing, market-size,
  platform, app-store, or model-provider claims.
- Separate facts, estimates, assumptions, and recommendations.
- Prefer primary sources for pricing, product claims, platform rules, and public
  documentation.
- Keep market outputs out of native C++ behavior unless translated into a clear
  product requirement.

## Workflow

1. Define the decision: segment, feature, price, launch channel, retention,
   model cost, or platform strategy.
2. Gather current evidence and cite sources.
3. Score options with criteria such as user value, technical feasibility,
   differentiation, cost, compliance risk, and time to learn.
4. Convert recommendations into measurable experiments or product requirements.
5. State what would change the recommendation.

## Product Lenses

- Creator workflow: fast recording, preview, MIDI export, DAW handoff.
- Music education: pitch/rhythm feedback, simple accompaniment, notation.
- Songwriting: chords, arrangement ideas, lyric alignment.
- Pro workflow: MusicXML, MIDI, catalog generation, batch/benchmark tooling.
- Mobile capture: low-friction recording, privacy, battery, offline behavior.

## Acceptance

- Claims have dates and sources.
- Recommendations include assumptions and measurable next steps.
- Experiment plans include target user, hypothesis, metric, and stop condition.
